Summer Work

I worked for one summer at a logistics firm called BAX in their Phoenix office, specifically with the group that coordinated with contractors that handled international freight arriving or departing by air. My primary role was maintaining both physical and digital records on the contractors' certifications, contract compliance, and a number of other things. Most of what I did was updating a pre-established database and generating reports for other members of the team.
The next summer I worked for the consumer loans section of a regional bank in Michigan. They had been maintaining all of their files physically, and as the bank grew it was becoming problematic. I mentioned that I had worked with databases the previous year and they asked me to build something for them. To that point I had never even attempted something like that, but I agreed to try. I was able to build everything they needed including putting all of their form letters and internal reports together and splitting the back end from the front end. After I left they hired someone with more expertise and she ended up rebuilding it a bit to make it more scalable and efficient.
